Why Goto Islands?

A Secret Hideaway
for Digital Nomads

Floating at the western edge of Japan, the Goto Islands are a wondrous place that brings space back to your heart, far from the city noise.
Surrounded by warm people, vast nature, and the blessings of sea and mountains, unhurried time flows gently here.
Snorkeling, trekking, fishing — a place to reconnect with nature and find yourself again.

Escape to

Quiet, Ghibli Islands.

An Island That Welcomes Everyone

The Goto Islands have long embraced diversity — alongside traditional Shinto and Buddhism, Hidden Christians fleeing persecution also settled here. It is a place where people continue to come together, beyond differences in culture and religion.
From this convergence was born a unique culture and history, and together with untouched nature, it creates a special atmosphere found nowhere else in Japan.
And now, drawn by its charm, more and more people are choosing to leave the city and live on Goto while working remotely.

Blue skies straight out of a Ghibli film, sparkling seas, warm-hearted people
— Take a breath, and find your way back to yourself.

Access

Accessible from major cities in Japan. Flights and ferries available from Nagasaki and Fukuoka.

From Tokyo

  1. Haneda Airpor → Fukuoka Airport: about 2 hours
    Then taking plane or ferry from Fukuoka

From Nagasaki

  1. Nagasaki Airport → Goto Fukue Airport: about 30 min
  2. Nagasaki Port → Fukue Port: about 1.5 hours (by high-speed ferry)

From Fukuoka

  1. Fukuoka Airport → Goto Fukue Airport: about 40 min (by plane)
  2. Hakata Port → Fukue Port: about 8.5 hours (by night ferry)

On the Island

  1. Rental car, bus, or bicycle are the main ways to get around on the islands.
